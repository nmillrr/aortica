Clinical Mission

Built for the hard cases

Cardiologists are highly skilled at routine ECG interpretation — but certain patterns are reliably difficult for even experienced readers. Aortica's identity is an AI ECG copilot that catches exactly those.

Every feature — from edge deployment to federated learning — serves one goal: a copilot that is accurate on the hard cases, trustworthy in its confidence estimates, explainable in clinical language, and accessible everywhere cardiologists practice.

  • Edge-case arrhythmias

    Intermittent pre-excitation (WPW), Brugada type 2/3, subtle atrial flutter with variable block, junctional rhythms masquerading as sinus, fascicular VT.

  • Strain patterns

    Early LV strain from hypertension or aortic stenosis, RV strain in PE (S1Q3T3), Takotsubo vs. ACS, and infiltrative cardiomyopathies.

  • Hiding in plain sight

    De Winter T-waves, Wellens syndrome, aVR ST-elevation, subtle QTc prolongation, and Sgarbossa criteria in paced/LBBB rhythms.

  • Risk signals

    ECG-derived markers of future AF onset, subclinical LV dysfunction, and progressive conduction disease before symptomatic block.
